Life as a Computer Science Professional

After putting in the effort to earn your Computer Science degree, you're well-prepared to enter the workforce. Numerous companies are actively seeking computer scientists, spanning across a wide range of industries. Virtually every company and field requires the expertise of computer science. Opportunities such as software development, database management, cybersecurity, and computer architecture are just a few examples of potential career paths.


Once you are employed, the learning does not stop. There are always new technologies to learn in any computer science field. For example, in cybersecurity, there are a host of different certifications that you can earn to further your skills and increase your pay in some instances. ISC2 is both a provider and a great resource for certifications.
These are a few things to expect in the real computer science workforce
  • Team Programming
  • Requirements Creation
  • Code Testing
  • Code Reviewing
  • Documentation
